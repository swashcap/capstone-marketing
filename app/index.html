Introduction Capstone

A WordPress theme especially designed for photography portfolio websites.

Download the Theme Visit the Demo

Packed Full of Features

Desktops, Phones, In-between

The theme looks great on mobiles, tablets, and even home PCs. Using responsive design, content is displayed in the best manner so you’re able to reach out to your visitors no matter how they visit your site.

Easily Organize Your Work

Capstone leverages the power of WordPress to make creating and editing your portfolios easy. Have a lot of great work? Group related items together with categories for a better experience.

Designed For Pictures

Images are first class citizens. The theme lets them shine.

What Did They Say?

Visitors need to be able to trust you. That’s why client testimonials play a central role in Capstone. Testimonials are easy to control and reinforce your outstanding qualities.

Always Be Converting

The main goal of your website is to get clients! Capstone features callout areas designed to move visitors to the your Contact page.

A Solid Foundation

The theme’s code is written and organized to be highly readable and easy to customize. It’s also completely free and open source.

And Much More…

The theme takes advantage of the WordPress platform with custom page templates, custom widgets, special theme options, page content customization, social media integration, and easy contact form setup. It’s designed from the ground up to make content fully flexible and editable.

Need more customization? Check out the pro version.

Capstone Pro

More customization and additional features. Coming soon.

The premiere version of Capstone offers more control over the theme’s appearance. It’s currently under development, and builds upon Capstone’s great platform. Included features:

  • Upload a logo image to the header
  • Easily select fonts from Google’s webfont selection
  • Choose from pre-configured color schemes or create your own to further customize the look and feel
  • Display portfolio images in a slideshow
  • Use shortcodes to integrate theme features directly in posts
  • Have multiple authors on the blog
  • Use WordPress’s post formats feature to differentiate between posts
